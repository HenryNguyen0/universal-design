What

What is Universal Design?

Universal Design is a approach to creating products and interfaces that work for everyone, accessible by anyone no matter their ability. The core principles include equal use, flexibility, and simple intuitive design. These principles ensure that digital products are accessible to all users, whether they're using assistive technologies, have different levels of technical expertise, or face temporary or permanent disabilities. A well-designed website should work equally well for someone using a screen reader, keyboard navigation, or traditional mouse input.

Norman's Design Concepts

Norman's Design Concepts complement these principles by focusing on intuitive user interaction through visibility, feedback, and constraints. Good design makes features clearly visible, provides immediate response for user actions, and prevents errors through appropriate constraints. A form should clearly show required fields, confirm when information is successfully submitted, and prevent users from submitting incomplete or incorrect information. When combined with Universal Design principles and accessibility guidelines, these concepts create interfaces that accessible and usable by anyone.

Website Accessibility

Website accessibility, guided by WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines), focuses on four essential principles: perceivable, operable, understandable, and robust. This means that content must be presented in ways that users can understand, like providing text alternatives for images. Users must be able to operate the interface through keyboard navigation. Content must be understandable with clear instructions, and the code must be robust enough to work with current and future assistive technologies. These guidelines are crucial for ensuring digital equality and often required by law in many countries.
